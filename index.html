About

I grew up in greater Boston and am currently studying Applied Mathematics and Chemical Engineering at Dartmouth. Most of my time goes to the overlap between quantitative research, markets, and machine learning. I've been working on deep-learning pipelines on neural data in the Touboul Lab at Brandeis, running independent quant research on USD/JPY and Japanese non-ferrous metals equities, and recently wrapped the Tuck Business Bridge with a DCF valuation of Cricut I'm genuinely proud of. I'm drawn to the places where careful modeling meets live markets and to the patient, craft-level work it takes to make them meet well.

Featured Projects

ER-Systematic

A systematic equity-research toolkit built around FX-driven arbitrage signals. Implements the USD/JPY × Japanese non-ferrous metals thesis end-to-end: data ingest, HAC-robust OLS beta estimation, and Random Forest directional models for names like Sumitomo, Mitsubishi Materials, and Mitsui Mining.

Python Pandas Scikit-Learn Statsmodels
NFL-Odds

An odds-modeling engine for NFL markets. Pulls lines and game data, fits probability models against market-implied prices, and flags situations where the book's edge looks thin. Part research project, part fan hobby, all about finding where Vegas might be wrong.

Python NumPy Regression Sports Analytics
10k-NLP-engine

An NLP pipeline for parsing SEC 10-K filings at scale. Segments and cleans the messy EDGAR disclosure format, then extracts tone and risk-language signal from Items 1A and 7, producing features usable in downstream equity models.

Python NLP SEC EDGAR PyTorch
